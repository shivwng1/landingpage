6. Data Retention and Deletion

6.1 Retention Principles

We retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements. Our retention practices are based on the following principles:

  • Data minimization: We retain only the information that is necessary for our legitimate business purposes
  • Purpose limitation: We retain information only for the specific purposes for which it was collected
  • Proportionality: The retention period is proportionate to the sensitivity and nature of the information
  • Regular review: We regularly review our retention practices and delete information when it is no longer needed

6.2 Specific Retention Periods

Different types of information are retained for different periods:

  • Voice recordings and call data: Retained for up to 3 years for service improvement and quality assurance
  • Account and billing information: Retained for the duration of the business relationship plus 7 years for legal and accounting purposes
  • Marketing and communication data: Retained until you unsubscribe or withdraw consent, plus up to 2 years for suppression purposes
  • Technical logs and system data: Retained for up to 2 years for security and operational purposes
  • Support and customer service records: Retained for up to 5 years for quality assurance and legal compliance
  • Employment records: Retained in accordance with applicable employment laws and regulations
  • Legal and compliance records: Retained for periods required by applicable laws and regulations

6.3 Data Deletion and Anonymization

When the retention period expires or when you request deletion of your information:

  • We securely delete or anonymize your personal information using industry-standard methods
  • Backup copies are deleted according to our standard backup retention schedules
  • Anonymized or aggregated data may be retained indefinitely for research and analytics purposes
  • Certain information may be retained longer if required by law or for legitimate business purposes such as fraud prevention
